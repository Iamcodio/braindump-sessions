### Early Internet Culture Research (Complete History)

**BBS Era (1978-1995):**
- First BBS: CBBS (Ward Christensen & Randy Suess, Chicago 1978)
- Technical: 2400 baud dial-up, one user at a time, text-only ANSI art
- Culture: SysOp absolute authority, warez scene, phreaking, FidoNet global network
- Peak: 100,000+ BBSes worldwide mid-1990s
- Death: 1995 - Web browsers + affordable ISPs killed dial-up overnight
- Survivors: ~900-1000 active via Telnet/SSH (2022)

**IRC Era (1988-present):**
- Created: August 1988, Jarkko "WiZ" Oikarinen, Finland
- Networks: EFnet (1990), Undernet (1992), DALnet (1994), Freenode (2003)
- Peak: 10 million users 2004-2005
- Current: ~350k users (2021)
- Cultural impact: Gulf War 1991 live reporting, hacker coordination, open-source projects
- Legacy: Slack, Discord, Teams inherit real-time text, channels, roles, bots

**Something Awful (1999-present):**
- Founder: Richard "Lowtax" Kyanka
- Business model: $9.99 registration fee (2001) = quality control through commitment
- Cultural exports: "All Your Base," Christopher Poole (before 4chan), early meme culture
- Philosophy: Paid registration = commitment filter, gatekeeping = quality maintenance

**YTMND (2001-2016):**
- Founded: Max Goldberg, April 2004
- Format constraint: Single looping image/GIF + single looping audio + text caption
- Peak memes: Picard Song, Blue Ball Machine, "One Does Not Simply..." (2004)
- Peak: Mid-2000s, 4 million visitors/month
- Shutdown: August 2016, relaunched 2020 (limited)
- Legacy: Constraint breeds creativity, looping format, absurdist humor

**4chan (2003-present):**
- Founded: October 2003, Christopher "moot" Poole (age 15)
- Structure: No registration, anonymous posting, threads 404 after inactivity
- Philosophy: "The site has no memory," ideas judged on merit not identity
- Memes spawned: LOLcats, Rickrolling, Pepe, Wojak, Rage comics
- Peak: 22+ million unique monthly (2022)
- moot departure: January 2015, sold to Hiroyuki Nishimura
- Legacy: Rapid meme iteration, Darwinian selection, also toxic culture

**Early Meme Timeline:**
- 1982: Emoticon ":-)" (Scott Fahlman)
- 1996: Dancing Baby
- 2001: "All Your Base Are Belong To Us"
- 2004: "One Does Not Simply" (Boromir)
- 2006: LOLcats explosion
- 2007: Rickrolling
